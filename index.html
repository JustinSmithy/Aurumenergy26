Aurum Energy — Logistics Quick Reference Guide
OOCAs this part of Aurum becomes active, we are aiming to get the script enabled so the commands in this guide will be activated by developers/LFM. Until then it is purely roleplay!
Introduction

This guide provides an overview of fundamental concepts, safety practices, operational guidelines and procedures for Aurum Energy's Logistics division. This gives you information on the standard steps for the main procedures within the logistics division. This list is not exhaustive and you will be asked to do more than just these tasks.

📋 COVERED PROCEDURES
Gas Station Refuel · Customer Cistern Refill · Transportation of Large Equipment
⚠️ NOTE
This guide does not constitute a certification. You should already hold your commercial driver's license and be certified to handle oversized loads and dangerous substances.
Ground Rules
RULE 1 · SAFETY FIRST
Personal safety is ALWAYS priority number one. You work in proper and appropriate PPE at all times.
RULE 2 · PROFESSIONALISM
You represent Aurum Energy and in extension Aurum Corporation in a professional manner while on the job. Opinions outside of work should not harm your employer and/or the corporation.
RULE 3 · INCIDENT REPORTING
Incidents are reported to your superiors and HR immediately. This includes: workplace accidents, harassment of any kind, robberies / GTA, and anything else you feel is reportable.
RULE 4 · RADIO DISCIPLINE
You are tuned into the Operations frequency /setfrequency slot(#) 441121 on your Aurum supplied radio. While working, radio in to Control at every location you work at.
RULE 5 · CONDUCT & DISCIPLINE
Reprimands and disciplinary action can be taken if gross misconduct is noticed. Any disciplinary measure can be contested by filing a complaint with HR via the intranet after the job is done.
Gas Station Refuel — Standard Procedure
STEP 1 · PLANNING
Plan your route using freeways as much as possible. Choose the route with the shortest use of surface streets. As you're dealing with dangerous substances, this is the best operating procedure considering safety — both road and environmental.
STEP 2A · PREPARE TRUCK
Grab keys to an MTL Tanker. Safety checklist: Windshield (no cracks) · Tires (tread over 4/32", no damage/deformation) · Lights & Indicators. Once confirmed safe, place the appropriate hazard shield on the front grille.
STEP 2B · FILL CISTERN
Proceed to the designated pumps at the refinery. MTL Tankers are bottom-loaded. Park beside the pump, unload hose from the side, attach to truck first then pump. Operate the pump, monitor for overfills, stop pump, detach and stow. /fuel truck check · /fuel truck refill pump
STEP 3 · DRIVING
Keep to your planned route. Radio in any difficulties deviating from the planned route.
STEP 4 · REFILLING
Scan and clear the work area on approach. Locate the gas station's separate refill point — do not block customer pumps. If the owner is present, greet them professionally. You represent the company — be polite and patient. For immediate assistance, contact Dispatch on the radio.
STEP 5 · RETURN & REPORT
Return to the refinery. Write a brief report: which gas station was fueled, state of vehicle used, any issues requiring servicing. Submit via the Delivery Log on this portal.
Customer Cistern Refuel — Standard Procedure
STEP 1 · COMMUNICATION
Requests usually come in via landline and are relayed to logistics. Call the business or person that put in the request. /phonedirectory
STEP 2 · REFINERY INTAKE
Cistern refills happen at the refinery fuel pumps. Inform security of the incoming customer truck so they are expected and let in. Gates: L on foot · H in vehicle. You do not drive the customer's truck — escort them to the pumps and operate the hoses yourself. The customer cannot assist unless in proper PPE.
STEP 3 · COMPLETION
Remind the customer to display the appropriate dangerous goods classification shield. Escort them off the premises and lock the refinery gates. Report the job done and include any anomalies.
Transportation of Large Equipment — Standard Procedure
OOCThese operations can be run as events with multiple staff. Feel free to roleplay in trimmed-down form too — just keep it properly roleplayed and realistic.
STEP 1 · PLANNING
Arrange: personnel availability, route, escort necessity, security detail. Jobs like these come down through the chain — a foreman or higher will request your assistance.
STEP 2 · VEHICLE INSPECTION
Aurum's fleet is stationed at the Palmer-Taylor Refinery. All vehicles must be inspected and prepared before departure to ensure the operation proceeds without issues.
STEP 3 · BRIEFING
The leading employee (usually a foreman) briefs everyone on the route, objective and roles. Questions and clarification happen here. Once everyone is clear, mount up and execute.
STEP 4 · EXECUTION
The foreman communicates on the radio throughout, updating everyone per stage. Keep the frequency clear and radio chatter minimal. We do not use radio codes — normal language only. Jargon is natural, but be prepared to explain to newer staff. OOC: These operations can count as multiple shifts at the foreman's discretion.
STEP 5 · RETURN
Once the assignment is complete, all personnel converge back at the refinery.

GROUND RULES (ALL FIELD OPERATIONS)

Personal safety is always priority 1. Proper PPE at all times.
RADIO IN

INCIDENTS

COMMANDS

⚡ Electric Car Charger — Maintenance Procedure
STEP 1 · COMMUNICATION
Radio the control room. Report your location and the work you will be doing.
STEP 2 · WORKSITE MANAGEMENT
Check for hazards: loose pavement, electrical hazards, fire hazards, presence of shady individuals, time of day. If hazards cannot be cleared — report and leave.
STEP 3 · RIGIDITY CHECK
Visual inspection: bolted securely to concrete base · housing intact · service panel secured · no loose wires or connectors visible.
STEP 4 · GRAB YOUR TOOLS
Retrieve your service kit and required tools before proceeding.
STEP 5 · ISOLATE THE CHARGER
Open the service panel (key is in your service kit). Flick the isolation switch.
STEP 6 · DIAGNOSE (OOC: use your character's certifications, Google if needed)
Check power with a line tester · inspect convertor for overheating/loose screws · measure voltage with multimeter on DC (large chargers output 480V).
STEP 7 · SERVICE / FIX
Examples: install new wiring · replace a convertor · adjust voltage (refer to the charger's service manual — multiple models exist).
STEP 8 · TEST (optional)
Connect your work van or truck to the charger and confirm it is functional and recognised.
STEP 9 · LOCK UP & CLEAN UP
Close and lock large service panel · flick isolation switch back · close and lock main panel · stow tools · clean worksite.
STEP 10 · REPORT
Describe which charger you worked on, what you found, and what components were used. Submit via Field Report on this portal.
💨 Wind Turbine (In Operation) — Inspection Procedure
⚠️ SAFETYMention the turbine is active and which one when radioing in. Aurum is not liable for injuries due to late-arriving help if you skip this step.
STEP 1 · COMMUNICATION
Radio control room with your location, the work you are doing, and specifically that the turbine is active and which one.
STEP 2 · VISUAL INSPECTION OF WORK SITE
Ensure the area you will be working in is clear and safe.
STEP 3 · VISUAL INSPECTION OF TURBINE
Check tower, turbine and blades for irregularities. Report severe issues. Radio control room to shut the turbine down if needed — or turn it off yourself at the control panel if possible.
STEP 4 · CHECK UP CLOSE
Tower/Base: Corrosion · Structural issues — Electrical: Components · Wiring — Blades: Cracks · Lightning Damage — Note: Turbine/Nacelle is not checked while in operation (see Static procedure)
STEP 5 · CMS (Condition Monitoring System)
Check internal monitoring values or ask control room: Vibration · Temperature · Power output anomalies · Wind loads · Rotor performance · Operational time vs. expectancy. Diagnose issues and focus further inspection on findings. OOC: CMS runs 24/7 — use it as the reason your character is there.
STEP 6 · INFRARED VIDEO (drone — adhere to drone laws)
Set up infrared camera on solid base. Record tower, turbine and blades. Inspect footage for hidden defects (cracks etc.) — can be reviewed at the office.
STEP 7 · REPORT
Describe which turbine, what was found, what was done. Have a foreman review so components can be ordered and a follow-up work order created. Submit via Field Report.
🔧 Wind Turbine (Stationary) — Full Service Procedure
📋 NOTEYou must be certified to climb a wind turbine tower.
STEP 1 · COMMUNICATION
Radio control room. Report location, work intended, that the turbine is inactive and which one.
STEP 2 · VISUAL INSPECTION OF WORK SITE
Ensure the area is clear and safe before approaching.
STEP 3 · VISUAL INSPECTION OF TURBINE
Check tower, turbine and blades for irregularities. Report severe issues.
STEP 4 · CHECK UP CLOSE
Tower/Base: Corrosion · Structural issues — Electrical: Components · Wiring — Turbine/Nacelle: Gearbox · Generator · Brakes — Blades: Cracks · Lightning Damage
STEP 5 · CMS CHECK
Same CMS values as the In Operation procedure. Diagnose and focus inspection accordingly.
STEP 6 · INFRARED VIDEO
Set up infrared camera. Record tower, turbine and blades. Inspect for hidden defects.
STEP 7 · INSPECT BLADES
Ultrasonic testing (detect hidden cracks before they reach the blade surface) · Acoustic emission testing (sensitive device listens for structural stress sounds including metal cracking).
STEP 8 · FIX ISSUES FOUND
Small: Replace wiring · Replace breaker switches — Medium: Report turbine inefficient — Large: Report turbine defective (shut down) · Replace blades · Replace nacelle (or parts of it)
STEP 9 · REPORT
Write up what turbine was worked on, what is done, what is still needed. Submit via Field Report.
☀️ Solar Collectors / Panels — Installation & Maintenance
STEP 1 · COMMUNICATION
Radio control room. Mention the building or location of the solar generators you will be installing or maintaining.
STEP 2 · WORKSITE INSPECTION
Ensure the area is clear and safe. Schedule for another time if: threatening presence of people, incompatible weather/atmospheric conditions. If working on a roof, ensure a colleague is available to assist.
STEP 3 · WORK ORDER CHECK
New installs: determine best transformer & panels for desired power output · determine most efficient orientation (south for max power · west for later-day household efficiency). Existing: check reported issues and maintenance schedule.
STEP 4 · TOOLS & MATERIALS
Checklist: Harness · Multimeter · Basic insulated tools · Aluminium frame components · Solar collectors/panels · Transformer(s)
STEP 5 · VISUAL INSPECTION
Check the rig: Transformer · Loose wiring/cables · Dirt and grime · Damage
STEP 6 · LOCKOUT TAGOUT (LOTO)
Turn the transformer off and confirm the rig is safe to work on.
STEP 7 · SERVICE
Fix determined issues · Diagnose transformer problems · Upgrade transformer firmware · Clean solar panels · Tighten/replace rig bolts
STEP 8 · TEST
Make sure the solar collectors work correctly before leaving.
STEP 9 · CLEAR WORKSITE
Gather tools · collect packaging materials of used components to dispose of back at base.
STEP 10 · REPORT
Write up a report on the work done. Submit via Field Report on this portal.
🏭 Substation — Inspection & Maintenance
Substations are essential to the state's power supply. Our security partner Arcani patrols and secures them. Always confirm the substation is safe to work at before beginning. They convert power: High Voltage (HV) → Medium Voltage (MV) → Low Voltage (LV).
STEP 1 · COMMUNICATION
Radio control room. Report your location and the substation you will be working at.
STEP 2 · WORKSITE INSPECTION
Ensure the area is clear and safe. Clear hazards or reschedule — examples: threatening presence of people, incompatible weather.
STEP 3 · CHECK FOR DAMAGE
Substations are susceptible to vandalism and weather. Confirm fencing is locked and intact. Check infrastructure inside for marks of damage from vandalism, lightning, weather. Check for missing copper wire.
STEP 3a · REPORT DAMAGE IF PRESENT
Report vandalism to Arcani immediately and advise further. Include in your final shift report.
STEP 4 · INSPECT THE SUBSTATION
Check: Noise irregularities (vibrations) · Source of burning smell · Circuit breakers · Relays · Transformers (oil level coolant/insulator/anti-oxidant · oil quality · UV) · Bushings · Fire suppression systems (CO2 tanks) · Emergency lights
STEP 5 · MEASURE SYSTEMS
Checklist: Breaker resistance · Insulation resistance (use Megger — tests resistance between two points)
STEP 6 · FIX / SERVICE
Service and fix issues found in previous steps by replacing parts and refreshing liquids.
STEP 7 · SIMULATE
Simulate scenarios to test automated protections.
STEP 8 · CLEAN
Ensure the substation is clean and functional. Remove tools and dispose of packaging from newly installed components.
STEP 9 · REPORT
Write your report. Advise on upcoming maintenance. Submit via Field Report on this portal.
🛢️ Oil Well — Maintenance Procedure
STEP 1 · COMMUNICATION
Radio control room. Report your location, the work you are doing, and specifically mention the oil derrick and which field (Grand Senora Desert or Murrieta Oil Field).
STEP 2 · WORKSITE INSPECTION
Ensure the area is clear and safe. Confirm the maintenance ladder is safe to use.
STEP 3 · REVIEW INSPECTION LOGS
Check if the specific well requires anything out of the ordinary before proceeding.
STEP 4 · GRAB YOUR TOOLS
Checklist: Harness · Basic tool chest · Torque wrenches · Cleaning materials · Grease gun and lubricants
STEP 5 · DIAGNOSE VISUALLY & AUDIBLY
Check for vibrations and stress/resistance noise from the derrick.
STEP 6 · LOCKOUT TAGOUT (LOTO)
Shut down the oil derrick and lock it in place before servicing.
STEP 7 · SERVICE
Bolts: Check loose/missing, torque up/replace · Cables & Pulleys: Report condition in service log, replace/schedule replacement · Corrosion: Clean surface, replace corroded parts, report rigidity concerns · Welds: Use NDT (non-destructive testing), refresh welds where needed · Lubrication: Lubricate moving parts with appropriate lubricants/grease · Clean oil residue and grime
STEP 8 · CLEAR WORK AREA
Ensure the work area is clean and clear before restarting.
STEP 9 · TURN OIL WELL BACK ON
Restore power and restart the derrick operation.
STEP 10 · DOUBLECHECK
Same as Step 5 — check for vibrations and stress/resistance noise outside of normal operation window.
STEP 11 · REPORT
Write up your report with recommendations for future work. Advise a work order for specific maintenance. Submit via Field Report on this portal.
Portal Standard Operating Procedures
SOP-01 · VEHICLE USAGE
All company vehicles must be logged before and after every shift. A Delivery Log or Field Report must be submitted each session. Unauthorised vehicle use is prohibited.
SOP-02 · SHIFT SYSTEM
All employees must clock in via the Shift System. Shifts without a corresponding log may not count toward quotas. Break times must be accurately recorded.
SOP-03 · COMMUNICATION
Professional radio etiquette at all times. Client communications must be logged. Do not discuss company info in public channels.
SOP-04 · INCIDENT REPORTING
Any on-road incident, vehicle damage, or interaction with emergency services must be reported within the same session. Failure to report is a disciplinary offence.
SOP-05 · METAGAMING POLICY
Information from portal logs must never be used in-game unless your character was present. Any breach results in immediate suspension.
SOP-06 · CONDUCT
Professional conduct is expected at all times. Uniform must be worn while on duty.
